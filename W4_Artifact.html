Five Human Factors of Big-Box Club Store Shoppers

Using Mode 3: Know People – 3.4 Five Human Factors Method

Eric Gonzalez | ACMP/CSCI 4260 – UX Design

Method Overview

The Five Human Factors method analyzes users through five lenses: Physical, Cognitive, Social, Cultural, and Emotional. This approach reveals deeper motivations, constraints, and behaviors beyond demographics, helping designers understand real human needs.

Physical

  • Large carts and bulk transport
  • Long walking distances
  • Heavy product handling
  • Spatial navigation challenges

Cognitive

  • Value optimization mindset
  • Price-per-unit thinking
  • Decision fatigue
  • Brand trust shortcuts

Social

  • Family/group shopping
  • Shared decision-making
  • Social proof via popularity
  • Business networking

Cultural

  • Bulk-buy culture
  • Membership identity
  • Efficiency values
  • Brand loyalty

Emotional

  • Satisfaction from savings
  • Security from stockpiling
  • Stress from crowds
  • Trust in product quality

User Needs Summary

Jobs

  • Stock homes and businesses
  • Reduce shopping frequency
  • Save money long-term
  • Access premium products

Gains

  • Lower per-unit pricing
  • Trust in quality
  • One-stop shopping
  • Efficiency

Pains

  • Crowded environments
  • Membership fees
  • Large package sizes
  • Navigation difficulty
